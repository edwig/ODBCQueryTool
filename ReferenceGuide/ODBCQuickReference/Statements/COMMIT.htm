COMMIT

Many databases support the 'COMMIT' command. Note however that this command is used in two separate and non-interchangable ways:

  • One: as a marker of the end of work, to make all actions in DML or DDL statements permanent in the database. In this case the command is used 'on its own'. Typically:

Statement 1;

Statement 2;

COMMIT;

Statement 3;

Statement 4;

COMMIT;

  • Two: as the marker at the end of work, of which the begin was marked by a 'BEGIN' statement. In this case the command cannot be issued 'on its own'. Typically:

BEGIN;

Statement 1;

Statement 2;

COMMIT;

BEGIN;

Statement 3;

Statement 4;

COMMIT;

COMMIT [WORK]
